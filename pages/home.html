Workshop Theme: Understanding of Secondary Organic Aerosols (SOAs)

This workshop aims to deepen our knowledge of secondary organic aerosols (SOAs) by focusing on their formation mechanisms, sources, and the intricate chemical pathways they undergo in the atmosphere. It seeks to address the underexplored aspects of SOA development, particularly the dynamic reactions involving volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and their atmospheric transformation.

Key Topics:

  • Formation Mechanisms of SOAs: An in-depth exploration of the chemical reactions driving the creation of SOAs from VOCs and other precursors.
  • Volatility and Transformation: A closer look at how SOAs evolve over time in the atmosphere and the environmental factors influencing their volatility and behavior.

Interactive Student Session:

  • Data Collection Methods: A review of the methods used to gather data, with a focus on the challenges encountered during field or laboratory work.
  • Innovative Analytical Techniques: Insights into cutting-edge techniques like GCMS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry), HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography), and AMS (Aerosol Mass Spectrometry) for differentiating between primary and secondary organic aerosols.
  • Health Impact Analysis: Delving into the tools and methodologies used to assess the human health effects of SOAs.
